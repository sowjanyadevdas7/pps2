        Task force moots regulatory body to implement NEP-2020 in Karnataka

        TNN | Nov 8, 2020, 08:05 IST

        TimesPoints

        Representative Image
        BENGALURU: A regulatory body, Karnataka Shikshana Aayog (KSA), is set to be formed to evaluate the progress of education in the state.
        Headed by the chief minister, KSA will ensure coordination among all education departments. The formation of KSA was one of several recommendations of the task force headed by former chief secretary SV Ranganath to prepare ‘Karnataka plan for implementing NEP-2020’. Karnataka will be the first state to come up with an implementation plan of the National Education Policy-2020.

        “This task force has considered all aspects of our state’s education system and programmes to come up with this plan. It will help us implement NEP in a holistic manner. We will discuss it with the chief minister and decide on the next step,” deputy chief minister Ashwath Narayan said.

        A proposed Karnataka Higher Education Commission (KHEC) will look at autonomous bodies that will govern and regulate higher education — Karnataka Higher Education Grants Council (KHEGS), Karnataka Higher Education Regulatory Council (KHERC) and Karnataka Research and Innovation Council (KRIC).
        For higher education, the proposal calls for focus on research, formation of regulatory bodies and faculty development programmes in the next 3-5 years. It has proposed implementation with specific targets mapped on a quarterly calendar for the next three years and a public review every quarter.         Bengaluru: Senior IPS officer withdraws resignation

        TNN | Nov 8, 2020, 07:59 IST

        TimesPoints

        80
        0 Added

        Visit TOI daily & earn TimesPoints!

        Chief minister BS Yediyurappa
        BENGALURU: Senior IPS officer P Ravindranath, who had tendered his resignation protesting the promotion given to his junior, has withdrawn it following the intervention of chief minister BS Yediyurappa.
        Additional general of police P Ravindranath said: “I met the chief minister on Friday who heard the matter and asked the chief secretary to address it.”
        Prior to this, Ravindranath met state police chief Praveen Sood who also asked him to withdraw resignation.
        Ravindranath tendered his resignation on October 28 after his batchmate T Suneel Kumar was promoted to the rank of director general of police (DGP) just before the latter’s retirement.
        This is the third time the 1989 batch IPS officer resigned in the last 12 years. The additional director general of police (ADGP) rank officer made headlines in 2014 while he was accused of clicking pictures of a woman in a coffee shop in Bengaluru. A case was also registered. Accusing the then police commissioner Raghavendra Audradkar of trying to implicate him, he submitted his resignation letter and later withdrew it. The case was also withdrawn later.
        In 2008, Ravindranath, who was then inspector general of police, resigned protesting alleged high-handedness of his senior, BE Umapathy. He alleged that Umapathy had rejected his leave application when there was a medical emergency at home. However, he withdrew it later.

        Karnataka: Cash-strapped transport department to lease out properties in 190 spots

        Sandeep Moudgal | TNN | Nov 8, 2020, 07:36 IST

        TimesPoints

        80
        0 Added

        Visit TOI daily & earn TimesPoints!

        Representative Image
        BENGALURU: With revenue sources drying up quickly amid the pandemic, the transport department is looking at various options, including leasing out land for construction of commercial complexes. Identified at 190 locations across the state, it will utilise 640 acres for the purpose.
        For long, Bangalore Metropolitan Transport Corporation (BMTC), Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC), North-East Karnataka Road Transport Corporation (NEKRTC) and North-West Karnataka Road Transport Corporation (NWKRTC) have been unable to generate expected revenue. Post the pandemic outbreak, the situation worsened with minimal use of public transport.

        “After analysing the possible mechanisms at our disposal, we decided to go in for public-private partnership (PPP) for developing these lands. The ownership will be with the government while the buildings will be built by private players,” transport department principal secretary Anjum Parvez explained.
        Having used prime properties for largely underutilised traffic and transit management centres, the government is now focussing only on commercial complexes beside its existing bus terminals.
        Officials said the properties will be given for a 30-year lease to the construction company, after which the government will take over. However, the companies will have to bid for a single tender that will see the government selecting only those which are ready to pay the highest value for leasing the land and constructing the complex. The department will approach the infrastructure development corporation of Karnataka (iDeCK) for a detailed study and construction valuation. At present, the finance department is vetting the PPP model proposal.
        With the 630-acre land valuation running anywhere between Rs 800 crore and Rs 1,000 crore, the government is expecting a substantial revenue share.
        Twenty of these locations are in Bengaluru, including Kempegowda bus stand in Majestic, and Shantinagar, Peenya and Magadi bus stations.
        While most of these plots have structures, including dilapidated bus stations which need to be demolished, 35 to 40 locations are vacant.
